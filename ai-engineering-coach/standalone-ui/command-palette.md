# Command Palette — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** The AI Engineer Coach has a lot of pages — Dashboard, Timeline, Output, Patterns, Anti-Patterns, Skills, Context Health, and more, grouped down the sidebar. To get somewhere, you normally scan the sidebar, find the right group, and click. That's fine once you know the layout, but it's slow when you're jumping around a lot or you don't yet remember where things live.

**The feature.** Press **Ctrl+K** (or **Cmd+K** on a Mac) from anywhere in the app, and a search box pops up in the centre of the screen with a list of every page underneath it. You start typing the name of where you want to go — "tim", "anti", "skill" — and the list instantly narrows to matching pages as you type. Hit **Enter** to jump to the highlighted one, or use the **arrow keys** to move the highlight and then Enter. **Esc** closes it without going anywhere. You can also just click an entry if you prefer the mouse.

**Why it's nice.**

- **Fast.** Two keystrokes and a few letters get you to any page — no hunting through the sidebar.
- **Keyboard-first.** You never have to lift your hands off the keyboard, which is how a lot of developers like to navigate.
- **No memorisation needed.** You don't have to remember which group a page lives in. You just type part of its name and it surfaces.
- **Discoverable.** Opening it shows the full list of pages at once, so newcomers get a quick map of everything the app offers.

**A quick scenario.** You're looking at your Dashboard and want to check your activity heatmap. Instead of finding the "Measure" group in the sidebar, you hit Ctrl+K, type "pat", see **Patterns** jump to the top of the list, and press Enter. You're there in about a second — and the palette has already vanished out of your way.

It's the same "quick switcher" idea you've probably met in tools like VS Code, Slack, or Notion — a single, familiar shortcut that becomes the fastest way to move around the app.
