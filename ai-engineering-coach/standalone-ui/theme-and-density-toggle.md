# Theme & Density Toggle — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** Everyone likes their tools to look a certain way. Some people want a bright, light screen; others want dark or a softer dimmed look that's easier on the eyes at night. Some want roomy, comfortable spacing; others want everything packed in tight so they can see more at once. Out of the box, the Coach app looks one way for everyone — and even if you could nudge it, the moment you reloaded the page it would snap back to the default.

**The feature.** A small **Appearance** control sits in the corner of the app. Open it and you can pick a **theme** — Dark, Light, or Dim — and a **density** — Comfortable or Compact. The whole app re-skins **instantly** as you click, so you can see the change and settle on what you like. Best of all, your choice **sticks**: close the tab, reload, or open the app again later and it comes back exactly how you left it — and because the preference is remembered by the app itself, it even follows you to another browser or device pointed at the same Coach.

**Why it's nice.**

- **Comfort.** Match the app to your lighting and your eyes — bright by day, dim or dark by night.
- **Your information density, your call.** Comfortable spacing to relax, or compact to fit more on screen.
- **Instant.** Changes apply live as you click, so there's no guessing.
- **It remembers.** Set it once and forget it — your look persists across reloads and sessions, not just until the next refresh.

**A quick scenario.** It's late and the bright default is straining your eyes. You open **Appearance**, switch to **Dim**, and the whole app softens immediately. You flip density to **Compact** so more of your timeline fits on screen. The next morning you reopen the Coach and it's still exactly the way you set it — no need to redo a thing.

It's the same personalization you expect from apps like VS Code, GitHub, or your OS settings — pick how it looks and feels once, and it stays that way.
