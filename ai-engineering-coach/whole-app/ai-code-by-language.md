# AI Contribution by Language — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** The Dashboard already hints at which languages the AI helped you write, but only as small text tags — you can see *that* Python and TypeScript show up, but not *how much* each one really added up to. There's no quick way to see, at a glance, where the AI did the heavy lifting across your tech stack.

**The feature.** A new chart card on the Dashboard ranks your languages by how many lines of code the AI wrote in each — your biggest contributors at the top, in a clean horizontal bar chart (showing roughly your top eight languages). Hover over any bar and a tooltip shows that language's exact line count and its share of the AI's total output. Like the rest of the Dashboard, it follows the filters at the bottom, so you can narrow it to a single workspace or time range and watch the ranking change.

**Why it's nice.**

- **At-a-glance ranking.** Instantly see which languages the AI contributed to most.
- **Real magnitude, not just presence.** The bar lengths show *how much*, not merely *which*.
- **Context for your stack.** Useful for spotting where you lean on the assistant heaviest.
- **Filter-aware.** Scope it to one project or month to compare how the mix shifts.

**A quick scenario.** You want to know where the AI helped you most this month. You glance at the Dashboard and the new chart makes it obvious — TypeScript is a long bar at the top, with Python and CSS trailing behind. You hover the TypeScript bar and see it accounted for over half of the AI's output. Now you know exactly which part of your stack is most AI-assisted.

It's the kind of "top contributors" bar chart you see in analytics dashboards everywhere — turning a pile of numbers into an instant ranking you can read in a second.
