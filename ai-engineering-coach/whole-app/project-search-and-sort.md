# Project Search & Sort — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** The Patterns page lists every project (workspace) you've worked in as a card — with its hours, request count, lines of code, and languages. That's great until you have a lot of them: finding one specific project means scrolling, and there's no way to ask "which projects did I spend the most time in?" without eyeballing the whole list.

**The feature.** A small toolbar appears above the project cards: a **search box** and a **"Sort by" dropdown**. Type into the search box and the list instantly narrows to projects whose name or languages match what you typed — results update live as you type. Use the dropdown to reorder the cards by **Hours**, **Requests**, **Lines of code**, or **Name**, so the biggest (or alphabetically first) projects jump to the top. If nothing matches your search, a friendly "no matches" message shows instead of an empty page. Everything you could already do on each card — like expanding it for more detail — keeps working exactly as before.

**Why it's nice.**

- **Find fast.** Jump straight to the project you care about instead of scrolling.
- **Rank instantly.** "Where did most of my time go?" is now one click on the sort dropdown.
- **Live and lightweight.** Filtering and sorting happen instantly as you type or choose — no waiting, no page reload.
- **Nothing lost.** The existing cards and their expand-for-detail behaviour are untouched.

**A quick scenario.** You want to see which side project ate the most of your week. You open the Patterns page, pick **Sort by → Hours**, and your biggest project floats to the top. Then you type "api" in the search box to focus on just your backend repos, and the list narrows in an instant.

It's the familiar search-and-sort you expect on any list of files, emails, or products — quietly making a long list manageable.
