# "Next Up" Achievement Spotlight — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** The Achievements page shows everything you've unlocked and everything still locked, but it's a passive scoreboard. It doesn't tell you the one thing people actually want to know: *which achievement am I about to get, and what's left to do?* You have to hunt through the locked list and compare progress bars yourself.

**The feature.** A new **"Next Up"** spotlight card sits near the top of the Achievements page, right under your latest unlock. It highlights the single locked achievement you're **closest** to earning — showing its icon, its tier (and tier colour), the XP it's worth, a progress bar, and how far along you are (e.g. "Almost there — 92%"). It surfaces the achievement's own description so you know exactly what it rewards. And if you've already unlocked everything, the card switches to a celebratory **"All achievements unlocked!"** state.

**Why it's nice.**

- **A clear goal.** Turns a wall of locked badges into one concrete "go get this next."
- **Motivating.** Seeing you're at 92% on something is a nudge to push the last bit.
- **Front and centre.** No more scanning the full list to find your nearest win.
- **Always relevant.** It updates as you make progress, so the spotlight follows your real momentum.

**A quick scenario.** You open the Achievements page to see how you're doing. Instead of scanning dozens of badges, the **Next Up** card immediately tells you you're 92% of the way to "Week Warrior." That's the little push you needed — you decide to get one more focused day in and claim it.

It's the same "you're almost there" prompt that game progression screens and loyalty apps use to keep a goal in sight — applied to your coding habits.
