# Night Owl Score — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** It's easy to lose track of *when* you're actually doing your AI-assisted coding. Are you a steady 9-to-5 worker, or do a lot of your sessions quietly creep into late nights and weekends? The Dashboard shows plenty of "how much" stats, but nothing that captures this work-life-balance angle at a glance.

**The feature.** A new **"Night Owl"** stat card joins the row of headline numbers at the top of the Dashboard. It shows the share of your AI coding that happens **outside normal working hours** — for example "37% Night Owl" — with a small subtitle pointing to your peak after-hours moment, like "peaks around 22:00." It uses the same filters as the rest of the Dashboard, so you can check a single project or date range and see whether that one was a late-night grind or a tidy daytime effort.

**Why it's nice.**

- **A gentle mirror.** One number tells you how much of your work spills into evenings and weekends.
- **Balance awareness.** Helpful for noticing creeping habits before they become burnout.
- **Specific.** The "peaks around 22:00" hint pinpoints *when*, not just *that* it happens.
- **Fits right in.** It sits alongside the existing Dashboard stats — no new page to visit.

**A quick scenario.** After a busy sprint you open the Dashboard and notice the **Night Owl** card reads 48%, peaking around 11pm. That's a wake-up call — nearly half your coding has been after hours. You filter to last week to confirm it wasn't a one-off, see it's a pattern, and decide to pull your sessions earlier in the day.

It's the same kind of "here's a habit you might not have noticed" nudge that screen-time and wellbeing trackers give you — a single honest number that prompts a small, healthy adjustment.
