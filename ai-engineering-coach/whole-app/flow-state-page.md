# Flow State page — what it feels like to use

**The problem it solves.** The Coach quietly figures out a lot about your deep-work rhythm — when in the day you do your best, most focused work, how that's been trending week to week, and how often you hit a real flow state — but right now there's nowhere to actually see it. That insight exists but stays hidden.

**The feature.** A new **Flow State** page in the sidebar brings it all into one view. At the top, a few headline numbers: your overall flow score (0–100), how quickly you tend to follow up while in the zone, and how many "deep-flow days" you've had. Below that, a **"best hours for focus"** chart shows which times of day you consistently do your strongest work, and a **weekly-trend line** shows whether your flow is climbing or slipping. Finally, a short list of plain-English **suggestions** tells you what to try next. Like every other page, it respects the filters at the bottom, so you can narrow it to a workspace or a date range.

**Why it's nice.**

- **Self-awareness.** You finally see *when* you focus best — and can protect those hours.
- **Trend, not just a snapshot.** The weekly line tells you if things are getting better or worse.
- **Actionable.** The suggestions turn the numbers into "do this next," not just a score to stare at.
- **No setup.** It's all computed from your normal usage — nothing to configure.

**A quick scenario.** You suspect you're sharpest in the late morning but fade after lunch. You open **Flow State**, and the "best hours" chart confirms it — a clear peak around 10–11am. You glance at the weekly trend, see it dipped last week, read the suggestion about long unbroken sessions, and decide to block your mornings for focused work going forward.

It's the kind of "here's your rhythm" view you get from a fitness or sleep-tracking app — except it's about how and when you do your best coding.
